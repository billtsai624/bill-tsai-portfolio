Part 02 — Successful Cases

Innovation in Action

Four projects representing the real-world impact of bridging material science, advanced manufacturing, and cross-functional collaboration.

Project 01

PC/PMMA Combo for Product Tiering

Utilized PC/PMMA composite materials to help the company create significant product differentiation between higher and lower-tier product lines — achieving premium 3D glass-like depth and metallic feeling without actual metal or NCVM processes.

Impact

Supports "Dead Front Light Effect". Built with 65% recycled materials — a significant sustainability milestone while delivering a distinctly premium aesthetic at scale.

Project 02

Advanced Keycap Development

Drawing on past experience with mobile phone frames, introduced Forging and Nano Molding Technology (NMT) to develop advanced keycaps — using a transparent plastic insert to successfully balance diverse departmental needs. Additionally developed a full metal keycap featuring a polished raised G logo — elevating the overall premium feel and tactile quality.

Impact

Enabled innovative backlit designs while maintaining metallic reflection. Coordinated multiple metal and plastic injection suppliers across GPM's low cost, ID's metallic feel, ME's plastic structure, and CMF's translucent letters — simultaneously.

Project 03

"Infinity Effect" Innovation & Internal Adoption

Led the "Infinity Effect" innovation project, utilizing transparent materials like PMMA combined with Inner Laser technology (green laser) to create multi-layered, floating logos and an infinite depth mirror effect.

Impact

Successfully applied the Infinity Effect on the Asuka and Quak products, sparking widespread discussion within the company. This led the internal team to begin deeper research and incorporate Inner Laser technology into the FY29 product roadmap.

Project 04

Plastic-Free Initiative & Rattan Innovation

To drive long-term "Structural Sustainability", searched for the next plastic-free step beyond recycled PC — turning to an almost forgotten natural raw material: Rattan. Led early rapid prototyping to significantly shorten validation time.

Impact

Successfully combined rattan's natural aesthetics with advanced manufacturing. Developed 3D thermoforming (>10mm depth), IMD, and GF bonding at 0.5/0.8mm thickness. Successfully prototyped on MX series mouse and keyboard concepts — demonstrating that traditional natural materials can achieve cutting-edge mass-production standards.

Project 05

Transparent Light with Inner Laser

Enhancing the visual effects of transparent materials by using Inner Laser technology and the principles of light-guiding. The laser-engraved pattern is embedded inside the material, amplifying the lighting effect to illuminate textures and logos — creating a sense of depth, elegance, and luxury.

Impact

Successfully demoed on the Foster Mini using a green laser (0.03mm focus length) on transparent materials including glass, crystal, PMMA, and PC/PET. Introduced the technology to GPM, designers, and marketing — generating broad internal interest and creating opportunities to integrate Inner Laser into NPI product roadmaps.
